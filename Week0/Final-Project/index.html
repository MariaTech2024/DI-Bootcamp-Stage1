Sir Winston Churchill

An inspirational statesman, writer, orator and leader who led Britain to victory in the Second World War

The photo was taken after Winston Churchill's speech about the World War II

Timeline

  • 1874

    Winston Leonard Spencer-Churchill was born at Blenheim Palace in Oxfordshire, England.

  • 1877

    After managing to get into the lowest class when he was 13, Winston's father didn't think that he was smart enough to study politics or law so he enrolled him in the army classes instead.

  • 1893

    Churchill enrolled in the army and joined the famous Sandhurst as an officer cadet.

  • 1895

    Winston became a cavalry officer in the 4th Queen's Own Hussars. He fought in both Sudan and India.

  • 1899

    Churchill goes to South Africa as a war reporter.

  • 1900

    At the general election, he became a Member of Parliament for Oldham and he made his first speech in Parliament in 1901.

  • 1908

    In his private life, he was married to Clementine and they went on to have five children.

  • 1910

    Churchill had become Home Secretary and was in charge of the Royal Navy. He then spent a short amount of time as Chancellor of the Exchequer, in charge of the country'staxes and money.

  • 1940

    He became prime minister, succeeding Neville Chamberlain. Churchill formed a national government and oversaw British involvement in the Allied war effort against the Axis powers, resulting in victory in 1945.

  • 1945

    After the Conservatives' defeat in the 1945 general election, he became Leader of the Opposition. Amid the developing Cold War with the Soviet Union, he publicly warned of an "iron curtain" of Soviet influence in Europe and promoted European unity.

  • 1951

    Churchill again became prime minister, remaining in office until his resignation on 5 April 1955. His second term was preoccupied with foreign affairs, especially Anglo-American relations.

  • 1953

    Winston Churchill was awarded the Nobel Prize for Literature.

  • 1964

    After leaving the premiership, Churchill remained a Member of Parliament until he stood down at the 1964 general election.

  • 1965

    Churchill suffered his final stroke on 10 January and died twelve days later on 24 January. He died aged 90. He is buried in a small churchyard in Oxford and his headstone reads: "Remember Winston Churchill".

"I now put Churchill, with all his idiosyncrasies, his indulgences, his occasional childishness, but also his genius, his tenacity and his persistent ability, right or wrong, successful or unsuccessful, to be larger than life, as the greatest human being ever to occupy 10 Downing Street." - Roy Jenkins concludes his biography of Churchill
